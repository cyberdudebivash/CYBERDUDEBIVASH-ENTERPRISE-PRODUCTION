🤝 Responsible Disclosure Policy — CYBERDUDEBIVASH®

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® is committed to maintaining the security and integrity of our platforms, tools, research infrastructure, and customer-facing services.

We believe that responsible disclosure is essential to improving security for everyone and welcome collaboration with ethical security researchers who act in good faith.

🎯 Purpose

This policy defines how security vulnerabilities affecting CYBERDUDEBIVASH® assets should be reported, handled, and disclosed in a responsible and coordinated manner.

It is intended to:

Protect users and customers

Enable timely remediation

Encourage ethical security research

Prevent harm caused by premature or malicious disclosure

📌 In-Scope Assets

Responsible disclosure applies to vulnerabilities affecting CYBERDUDEBIVASH®-owned and operated assets, including but not limited to:

www.cyberdudebivash.com

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® enterprise platforms and services

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® production security tools and scanners

AI-driven security automation systems

Official CYBERDUDEBIVASH® GitHub repositories

Research and threat intelligence infrastructure

🚨 How to Report a Vulnerability

Security issues should be reported privately and responsibly.

📧 Primary contact:
bivash@cyberdudebivash.com

Please include the following details:

Description of the vulnerability

Affected system, product, or repository

Steps to reproduce (if applicable)

Proof-of-concept (PoC), logs, or screenshots (if available)

Impact assessment and severity (if known)

Encrypted communication can be arranged upon request.

🕒 Disclosure Timeline

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® follows a coordinated disclosure approach:

Acknowledgement – We will acknowledge receipt of your report.

Assessment – The issue will be validated and assessed.

Remediation – Fixes or mitigations will be developed and deployed.

Disclosure – Public disclosure may occur after remediation or by mutual agreement.

Timelines may vary depending on complexity and severity.

🛡️ Researcher Expectations

We ask that security researchers:

Act in good faith

Avoid data exfiltration, destruction, or service disruption

Limit testing to what is necessary to demonstrate the issue

Do not publicly disclose vulnerabilities without coordination

Respect user privacy and data protection laws

Comply with all applicable local and international laws

🚫 Prohibited Activities

The following activities are not permitted and may result in legal action:

Exploitation beyond proof-of-concept

Denial-of-Service (DoS / DDoS) attacks

Social engineering or phishing attacks

Automated scanning without authorization

Physical security testing

Attacks against third-party systems not owned by CYBERDUDEBIVASH®

⚖️ Legal Safe Harbor

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® considers security research conducted in accordance with this policy to be authorized and in good faith.

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

Follow this Responsible Disclosure Policy

Avoid intentional harm

Provide sufficient time for remediation

Do not violate applicable laws

This safe harbor does not apply to malicious activity, extortion, or unauthorized access beyond reasonable testing.

🧠 Recognition & Collaboration

While CYBERDUDEBIVASH® does not currently operate a public bug bounty program, we value meaningful contributions and may:

Acknowledge researchers in advisories or release notes

Collaborate on coordinated disclosures

Explore future partnerships or research opportunities
