Program Scope

Only test assets listed in-scope. Testing out-of-scope assets will disqualify your submission.

✅ In Scope
  • cyberdudebivash.com (all subpaths)
  • intel.cyberdudebivash.com

  • tools.cyberdudebivash.com

  • Third-party services & integrations

  • Physical security attacks

  • Clickjacking on non-sensitive pages

  • Outdated browser / plugin vulnerabilities
  • Missing security headers (low-value only)

Submission Process

Follow these steps to ensure your report is processed quickly and fairly.

01
Discover the Vulnerability
Identify a security issue in our in-scope systems. Do not exploit beyond what is necessary to prove the vulnerability exists. Never access, modify, or delete user data.
02
Document Your Finding
Write a clear, reproducible report with exact steps to reproduce, affected URL/endpoint, screenshots or a PoC video, and your estimated CVSS score and impact assessment.
03
Submit via This Form
Use the secure submission form below. Encrypt attachments if they contain sensitive data. Include your contact email for reward payment details.
04
Triage & Validation
Our security team reviews your report within the stated SLA. We'll acknowledge receipt within 4 hours and provide triage results within the SLA for your severity level.
05
Reward & Recognition
Upon validation, we'll agree on a final CVSS score and reward amount. Payment is issued within 14 business days via bank transfer, UPI, or PayPal. Hall of Fame listing upon request.

Coordinated Disclosure Policy

CYBERDUDEBIVASH follows a 90-day coordinated disclosure policy aligned with industry standards (Google Project Zero, CERT/CC). After you submit a valid report:

  • Day 1 — Acknowledgement within 4 hours of submission
  • Day 1–7 — Triage, validation, and severity classification
  • Day 7–30 — Engineering patch development and internal testing
  • Day 30–60 — Patch deployment and verification across all environments
  • Day 90 — Coordinated public disclosure (CVE assignment if applicable)

If a patch requires more than 90 days, we'll discuss a timeline extension with you. We will not pursue legal action against researchers acting in good faith within this policy.
