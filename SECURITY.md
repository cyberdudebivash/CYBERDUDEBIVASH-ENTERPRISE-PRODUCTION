🔐 Security Policy — CYBERDUDEBIVASH®

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® takes the security of our platforms, tools, research infrastructure, and customer-facing services seriously.

This document outlines how to responsibly report security vulnerabilities and how we handle security issues across the CYBERDUDEBIVASH® ecosystem.

📌 Scope

This security policy applies to the following CYBERDUDEBIVASH® assets, including but not limited to:

www.cyberdudebivash.com

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® enterprise website and platforms

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® production security tools and scanners

AI-driven cybersecurity and automation systems

Public-facing APIs, services, and integrations

Official CYBERDUDEBIVASH® GitHub repositories

Research and threat intelligence infrastructure

🚨 Reporting a Security Vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability affecting any CYBERDUDEBIVASH® asset, we encourage responsible disclosure.

Please report security issues to:

📧 bivash@cyberdudebivash.com

When reporting a vulnerability, please include:

A clear description of the issue

Steps to reproduce the vulnerability (if applicable)

Affected component, service, or repository

Potential impact and severity assessment

Any proof-of-concept (PoC), logs, or screenshots (if available)

🛡️ Responsible Disclosure Guidelines

We kindly request that security researchers:

Do not publicly disclose vulnerabilities before coordination

Avoid exploiting vulnerabilities beyond proof-of-concept

Do not access, modify, or delete data belonging to others

Allow reasonable time for investigation and remediation

Act in good faith and comply with applicable laws

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® does not tolerate extortion, data exfiltration, or disruptive testing.

🔍 Vulnerability Handling Process

Once a report is received, CYBERDUDEBIVASH® will:

Acknowledge receipt of the report

Validate and assess the vulnerability

Determine severity and impact

Develop and deploy remediation measures

Coordinate disclosure where appropriate

Response timelines may vary depending on severity and complexity.

🧠 Research & Ethical Security Testing

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® actively conducts internal security research, testing, and threat modeling across its platforms.

We welcome collaboration with ethical security researchers and professionals who align with responsible disclosure principles.

⚠️ Out-of-Scope Activities

The following activities are out of scope and may result in no response or legal action:

Social engineering attacks against employees or partners

Physical security testing

Denial-of-Service (DoS / DDoS) attacks

Automated scanning without permission

Testing against third-party services not owned by CYBERDUDEBIVASH®

📜 Legal Notice

This policy does not grant permission to conduct unauthorized testing. All security research must comply with applicable laws and regulations.

CYBERDUDEBIVASH® reserves the right to take legal action against malicious or irresponsible activity.
